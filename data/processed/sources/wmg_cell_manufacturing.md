## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- University of Warwick Manufacturing Group (WMG) analysis of how cell manufacturing roles will evolve
- Conducted with UK/EU industry partnerships (likely Faraday Institution collaboration)
- Projects workforce composition shift: more design/engineering, fewer implementation specialists
- Critical for manufacturing_productivity and automation sensitivities

**Workforce composition shift:**
- **Design (engineering)**: 8% → 30% (+22 percentage points)
  - Includes: process engineers, R&D, digital twins, simulation, optimization
  - Driven by: increased process complexity, automation programming, quality systems
- **Implement (construction/startup)**: 50% → 21% (-29 percentage points)
  - Current: equipment installation, line setup, initial troubleshooting
  - Future: fewer hands-on role needed with modular/standardized equipment
- **Operate (production)**: presumably stable (10-12%)
- **Support (admin/logistics)**: presumably stable (8-10%)

**Implications for workforce planning:**
- ROLE-01 and ROLE-03 claims anchor on WMG finding to show workforce composition evolution
- Total headcount per GWh may remain stable, but skill mix shifts dramatically
- Training implications: need for more engineering talent, less for manual technician roles
- Regional impact: design roles more concentrated in R&D centers (likely fewer jobs distributed to manufacturing regions)

**Automation context:**
- Shift from 50% implementation to 21% reflects automation of equipment setup and startup tasks
- Increase in design (30%) reflects need for continuous process improvement and system customization
- Net effect: similar total labor intensity but much higher skill/wage profile

**Cross-references:**
- Complements faraday_gigafactory role split (design 50%, implement 30%, operate 10%, support 9%, other 1%)
- Faraday reflects current state; WMG projects future (2030+) state
- Suggests future facilities will look more like Faraday (higher design share) than current US plants

**Caveats and uncertainties:**
- WMG projections are aspirational (what UK industry WANTS, not necessarily what occurs)
- Assumes significant automation investment (high capex scenario)
- UK context may not reflect US/China dynamics
- Does not address potential for automation-resistant tasks (quality troubleshooting, training)
