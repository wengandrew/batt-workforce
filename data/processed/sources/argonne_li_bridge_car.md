## Extracted Quotes / Claims

> "CAR found the largest shortages in the upstream parts of the battery supply chain, like mining and refining, followed closely by downstream steps like battery cell manufacturing and pack assembly. The skills in shortest supply were chemistry, chemical engineering, battery management systems, and other skill sets that require a college or graduate degree."

## Notes

Comprehensive workforce assessment (BIETNA — Battery Industry Education and Training Needs Assessment) identifying critical skills gaps: chemistry, chemical engineering, battery management systems, and advanced technical roles. Emphasizes mismatch between industry growth and educational pipeline capacity. Recommended interventions: expanded apprenticeships, employer–academic coordination, curriculum alignment. Directly informs manufacturing_productivity and automation sensitivities regarding skill composition and availability constraints. Report identifies key engineering roles (electrical, manufacturing, automation, process) and operations roles (line operators, QA, supervisors) as demand drivers.
