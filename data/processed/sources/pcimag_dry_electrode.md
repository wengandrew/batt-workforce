## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- PCI Magazine article on wet vs. dry electrode coating technologies in battery manufacturing
- Key technology development: dry electrode coating eliminates the drying-oven stage
- Commercial validation: Tesla has scaled 4680 cells with dry-electrode (validated FY2025)
- Represents a material labor-reduction opportunity in electrode manufacturing

**Why dry-electrode matters:**
- Wet electrode coating requires: coating → drying oven (energy/time intensive) → collection
- Dry electrode coating: coating → direct collection (no separate drying step)
- Labor impact: drying oven stage is labor-intensive (monitoring, material handling, defect inspection)
- Estimated 15-25% of electrode production labor in wet process → directly eliminable with dry

**Not just labor cost savings:**
- BCG analysis noted labor = ~7% of cell cost (China context)
- Drying-oven elimination saves more than 7% because:
  - Labor elimination (yes, 7%)
  - Energy savings (oven thermal cost ~ 10-15% of cell energy)
  - Material throughput improvement (faster cycle time = more GWh from same equipment)
  - Space savings (smaller footprint = lower real estate cost)
- Total savings could be 15-25% of cost, not capped by labor-cost percentage

**Technological readiness:**
- No longer theoretical: Tesla 4680 cell production (FY2025) validates commercial viability
- Implies other manufacturers (CATL, LG, Samsung) likely to deploy dry electrode 2026+
- As technology diffuses, labor-intensity reduction will be one of several benefits

**Implications for energy_density sensitivity:**
- NEW-06 claim: dry-electrode is a labor-reducing process innovation
- Not dependent on energy density improvements; applicable to all chemistries
- Could reduce labor intensity by 10-20% as adoption spreads

**Cross-references:**

**Caveats and limitations:**
- PCI Magazine is trade press; technical claims should be cross-verified
- Dry-electrode scaling may face manufacturing yield challenges (not addressed in article)
- Energy density not directly improved by dry-electrode (electrode quality/form factor unchanged)
- Cost savings realization depends on equipment capex (likely high initial investment for new coating lines)
