## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- Miller & Chevalier law firm analysis of Omnibus Battery Manufacturing and Recycling Act (OBBBA, enacted 2025)
- Legal interpretation of how OBBBA modified IRA battery manufacturing incentives
- Critical for understanding post-2025 policy environment for battery workforce planning

**§45X manufacturing credit:**
- Status: SURVIVED (contrary to some fears of complete repeal)
- Changes:
  - Modified content requirements (more stringent on battery components)
  - Phase-out 2030-2033 (credit expires by end of 2033)
  - FEOC rules: Functional Exempt Originated Content allows some flexibility on critical minerals sourcing
- Implication: battery manufacturing remains subsidized through early 2030s, then cost structure must change

**§30D consumer tax credit:**
- Status: TERMINATED (effective 9/30/2025)
- Ended the $7,500 EV purchase tax credit (now expired)
- Implication: EV DEMAND may soften (vehicles ~$7,500 more expensive without credit), affecting battery demand growth post-2026

**§ATVM loan program:**
- Status: REPEALED (legacy advanced-tech manufacturing loan program)
- Originally: $25B in loan authority for EV/battery manufacturing
- Effective impact: limited (most critical projects already financed; repeal removes future loan option)
- Implication: large greenfield battery projects now rely solely on private capital + IRA tax credits (45X) + state incentives

**§48C clean energy investment credit:**
- Status: UNAFFECTED ($10B envelope continues)
- Link to workforce: 45X historically linked to "job quality" requirements; 48C continues this pattern
- Implication: tax credits still reward job-creation, but no minimum employment levels specified

**Implications for workforce scenario modeling:**
- POLICY-01 claim documents that IRA was "mixed, not wholesale termination"
- Post-OBBBA: 45X remains through 2030-2033, but demand-side weakness (no 30D) may reduce growth
- Supports SCEN-02 (moderate US growth scenario, not high-growth IRA-continuation scenario)

**Cross-references:**
- Directly supports csis_new_phase_2026 finding that 2025 saw investment cancellations (loss of 30D consumer credit driver)
- Aligns with mckinsey_demand_outlook_2025 "fading momentum" demand scenario
- Explains why US market-share projections are being downward-adjusted from 10-15% to 7-10%

**Limitations and caveats:**
- Legal interpretation of OBBBA; subsequent Treasury guidance or rulemaking may clarify/modify intent
- §45X phase-out 2030-2033 creates cliff risk (projects must be complete by 2033 to capture credit)
- §30D termination impact on EV demand uncertain (may be offset by cost reductions or other incentives)

**Workforce-specific analysis:**
- 45X modifications do NOT include explicit employment requirements (unlike some state programs)
- Means battery manufacturing workforce will grow based on capacity needs, not subsidy-linked job guarantees
- Training investments (Georgia Quick Start, Ohio CWDB) remain important for supply-side preparation

- Monitor project timelines: projects must be operational by 2033 to capture 45X credit
- Assess whether states increase incentives to offset federal credit phase-out
