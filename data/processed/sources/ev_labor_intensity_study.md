## Notes

**Authors**: Andrew Weng, Omar Y. Ahmed (both University of Michigan), Gabriel Ehrlich, Anna Stefanopoulou  
**Publication**: Nature Communications, Vol. 15, Article 8088 (October 2024)  
**DOI**: 10.1038/s41467-024-52435-x

This is Andrew Weng's Nature Communications paper, the core primary source for View A (labor intensity curve). The study directly contradicts the common industry narrative of "30% fewer workers for BEV assembly" by analyzing three real plants:

1. **Alameda County, CA (Tesla NUMMI site)**: Near steady-state BEV production >10 years; labor intensity remains ~3x higher than historic ICEV baseline
2. **Oakland County, MI (GM Orion plant)**: Chevy Bolt transition 2016-2023; workforce transitioned from ICEV to BEV production over 5 years
3. **McLean County, IL (Rivian site)**: Early-stage ramp-up of electric pickup truck production

Key quantitative findings:
- **Ramp-up multiplier**: >10x labor intensity vs. historic ICEV baseline
- **Mature BEV plant baseline**: ~3x higher than ICEV
- **Time to parity**: >15 years to achieve labor intensity equal to ICEV assembly
- **Implication**: Widespread rapid job loss is unlikely; instead, sustained employment boost during transition

Methodology leverages publicly available datasets: vehicle production data from Automotive News Research & Data Center, employment data from U.S. Quarterly Workforce Indicators (NAICS code 3361 — motor vehicle manufacturing). County-level analysis enables attribution to single assembly plant where applicable.

The paper flags the gap between technical assumptions and shop-floor reality — prior studies (BCG, academic models) underestimated EV labor intensity by assuming production process similarities to ICEVs without accounting for ramp-up complexity, quality control burden, and sustained learning curve.
