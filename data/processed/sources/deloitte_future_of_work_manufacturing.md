## Extracted Quotes / Claims
> "We see that workforce demand remains substantial overall, though the challenge is a shift from lower skill, repetitive, traditional production-line type roles (replaced by automation) to higher skilled roles. This is also where we see the largest gap in the workforce (skilled trade or journeyman level roles - maintenance, controls technician, manufacturing tech, production tech - increasingly more demand with a depleting workforce). State level workforce development programs in the US are starting to catch up with this need..."

> "Deloitte and The Manufacturing Institute anticipate the shortfall in US manufacturing during the next decade to reach the highest levels ever recorded, higher than the earlier estimates of 2 million unfilled jobs."

> "Technology is likely to create more jobs than it destroys—as it has done historically."

## Notes
Co-cited source with Manufacturing Dive, AMTEC, and Manufacturing Tomorrow for workforce shift claim. Introduces ten future manufacturing personas (Digital Twin Engineer, Predictive Supply Network Analyst, Robot Teaming Coordinator, Smart Factory Manager, Drone Data Coordinator, Smart QA Manager). Emphasizes "soft skills" (critical thinking, creativity, people management) as increasingly critical in automated environments. Quantitative claim on 2M+ job shortfall is important for sensitivity modeling.
