## Extracted Quotes / Claims
> "Tennessee lawmakers approved a $900 million incentive package for Ford in October 2021, including a $500 million reimbursement for construction work on the megasite. The remaining state contributions will foot the bill for a new $40 million college of applied technology and several infrastructure projects: $200 million to build a road interchange, $138 million for water treatment facilities and a wastewater line to the Mississippi River, $21 million for workforce training, $5 million for local government consulting."

> "To receive the full $500 million grant, Ford and BlueOvalSK (the joint venture battery company) must create at least 90% of the roughly 5,800 promised jobs in 10 years...The project overall is expected to generate 27,000 new jobs and $1.02 billion in annual earnings...Ford officials specified that the vehicle manufacturing plant will employ 3,000 of those workers, and the remainder will work at the property's BlueOvalSK battery plant."

> "Ford and SK On are partnering with the Tennessee College of Applied Technology to train students on electric vehicle and battery manufacturing and repair at a new, state-funded Stanton campus that opened in June. Ford also launched BlueOval Learning, a program that supports STEM curricula and manufacturing training in K-12 schools in addition to partnerships with colleges."

## Notes
Ford's BlueOval City plant represents major workforce development investment with state-funded training campus (TCAT Stanton) and STEM-focused educational partnerships. The $900M incentive package includes $40M college of applied technology, $21M workforce training, and $500M in construction reimbursement. Direct employment: ~5,800 jobs (3,000 at EV plant, 2,800+ at battery plant BlueOvalSK), with strict job creation targets (90% within 10 years) triggering clawbacks if missed. Federal loan of $9.2B through Inflation Reduction Act. TCAT Stanton campus opened June 2024 offering EV and battery manufacturing/repair training. BlueOval Learning extends STEM/manufacturing curricula to K-12 schools. Policy_scenarios elements include both financial incentives (grants, tax credits) and human capital infrastructure (state training campus). Projected 27,000 total jobs regionally with $1.02B annual earnings. Following production timeline delay to 2027, battery production expected late 2025.
