## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- Semiconductor Industry Association (SIA) workforce analysis from 2023
- Provides an ANALOG case study: semiconductor manufacturing faced similar workforce scaling challenges as batteries now face
- 67k shortage figure is used in Stage-5 as a comparison to battery manufacturing workforce needs

**Why semiconductors are relevant analog:**
- Both sectors: require similar skilled trades (equipment technicians, process engineers, maintenance specialists)
- Both sectors: rapid capacity expansion (fabs comparable to gigafactories in complexity/cost)
- Both sectors: compete for same talent pool (electricians, welders, mechanical technicians)
- Semiconductor experience (2020-2023) shows what barriers to expect for batteries (2024-2030)

**Bottleneck finding:**
- SIA emphasizes that shortage is NOT total headcount but SKILLS
- 67k gap primarily: CNC/machining technicians, electrical technicians, equipment maintenance specialists
- Implies that battery manufacturing will face similar skills bottleneck (not just absolute worker shortage)

**Implications for workforce planning:**
- ANALOG-01 claim: semiconductor sector provides precedent for skilled trades bottleneck
- Suggests battery manufacturing training/apprenticeship capacity is critical constraint
- If semiconductors faced 67k skilled-trades gap with 250k fab workers, batteries may face proportional gap as they scale

**Cross-references:**
- JLL report (jll_skilled_trades) provides complementary data on skilled trades shortage (4:1 trades:apprentice ratio, 82% shortage in boom markets)
- Both SIA and JLL highlight apprenticeship pipeline as constraint
- Suggests policy focus on training capacity (not just job creation) is essential

**Caveats:**
- SIA data from 2023; semiconductor market conditions may have shifted by 2026
- Semiconductor plants (fabs) have different skill requirements than battery plants (gigafactories), though significant overlap
- US semiconductor shortage reflects tight labor market generally, not unique to sector

**Limitations of analog:**
- Battery manufacturing is newer than semiconductors; learning curves may differ
- Battery plants may be more geographically concentrated (easier to build local training ecosystems)
- Semiconductor shortage partly due to visa/immigration restrictions; battery sector may face different barriers
