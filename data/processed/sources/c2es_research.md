## Notes

Comprehensive regional economic impact analysis (2026–2035) modeling battery supply chain expansion across Alabama, Georgia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Tennessee. Uses IMPLAN input-output framework with state-readiness indices to allocate investment and job creation.

**Key workforce findings**:
- 374,000 total jobs across the Southeast region over 10 years
- Job creation heavily weighted to construction (36%), then battery/materials manufacturing (28%), logistics/supply chain (16%)
- Cross-state spillover effects: 5–15% of job creation in each state driven by investment elsewhere in the region (largest in Georgia at 15%)
- Georgia leads in absolute employment (84,200), followed by North Carolina (73,200) and South Carolina (~56,900)
- Construction jobs front-loaded; manufacturing jobs sustained over production ramp-up

**Supply chain insights**:
- Analysis assumes 100% of U.S. battery demand met by domestic manufacturing (ambitious/supportive-policy scenario)
- Cell manufacturing accounts for largest economic output (~$69B); cathode active materials (~$37B)
- Emphasis on need to develop upstream (materials processing, component manufacturing) and recycling segments alongside current downstream focus

**Scenario assumptions**:
- Continuation of federal policy through early 2025 (Section 30D clean vehicle tax credit, manufacturing provisions)
- Does NOT reflect more recent policy changes that have moderated EV demand projections
- Interpretation: potential scale under supportive policy, not near-term forecast

**Methodology notes**: 
- Stakeholder engagement with industry survey (site selection, construction timelines, supply chain constraints) and regional economic development organizations
- Multi-Regional Input-Output (MRIO) framework captures cross-state supply chain linkages
- Over 70 individual indicators in state-readiness indices (industrial base, energy prices, transportation, workforce availability/cost)

**Follow-up extraction ideas**: 
- Detailed state-level employment by facility type/ramp-up timeline could inform workforce projection models
- Spillover effects data crucial for regional coordination scenarios
- Upstream segment emphasis supports energy_density and automation sensitivities (new materials processing skills, quality control scaling)
