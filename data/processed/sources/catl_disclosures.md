## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- CATL (Contemporary Amperex Technology Co. Limited) is the world's largest battery manufacturer
- Public statement on complexity of sodium-ion battery manufacturing development
- Critical for energy_density sensitivity: Na-ion is viewed as a lower-density alternative to Li-ion
- Indicates manufacturing process complexity may require additional workforce upskilling

**Data limitations:**
- Company statement of engineering challenges is qualitative ("100+ challenges") without quantitative labor impact
- CATL's global headcount is published in financial reports, but allocation to specific chemistry/product lines not disclosed
- No per-GWh labor intensity figures available for Na-ion specific production

**Implications for workforce planning:**
- Na-ion manufacturing may require MORE skilled labor than mature Li-ion lines due to engineering challenges
- Contradicts simple assumption that alternative chemistries = lower labor intensity
- Relevant to Stage-5 claim AUTO-03: "Na-ion is not a clean drop-in; additional engineering and process development labor required"

- Use as evidence that chemistry changes don't automatically reduce workforce needs
