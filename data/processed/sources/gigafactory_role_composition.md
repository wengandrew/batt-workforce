## Extracted Quotes / Claims

> "Each battery gigafactory typically creates: 2,000–5,000 direct manufacturing jobs; thousands of construction jobs during development; long-term supplier and logistics positions."

> "The role composition in a gigafactory requires a highly specialized workforce divided into engineering, operations, supply chain, and facilities. Because these plants handle massive, high-volume production, a large portion of the roles focus on automation, chemistry, and maintaining ultra-clean, dry-room environments."

> "High-demand roles include: Electrical Engineers; Manufacturing Engineers; Controls & Automation Engineers; Project Managers; Battery Technology Specialists; Power Systems Engineers."

> "Process & Manufacturing Engineers optimize electrode preparation, cell assembly, and finishing processes to maximize yield and minimize scrap. Automation & Controls Engineers design, deploy, and maintain robotic systems, PLC networks, and automated conveyor lines. Battery/Chemical Engineers monitor cell chemistries (e.g., LFP, NMC), cathode/anode composition, and energy density variations."

> "Labor Intensity: Up to 10× more labor-intensive per unit during the initial ramp-up phase. Key Roles: Process/Quality Engineers, Equipment Maintenance Technicians, and Training/Onboarding Specialists to deal with high scrap rates."

## Notes
