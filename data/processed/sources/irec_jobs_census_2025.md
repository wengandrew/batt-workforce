## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- IREC (Interstate Renewable Energy Council) annual jobs census covers clean energy employment
- 2025 edition includes new battery storage subsector breakdown
- Key finding: construction is majority (52.3%) of battery-storage jobs, not manufacturing
- Critical for defining scope: manufacturing_productivity sensitivity EXCLUDES construction per ICCT definition

**Sector breakdown implications:**
- Battery-storage total employment includes:
  - Construction (52.3%): site prep, equipment installation, wiring, testing
  - Manufacturing (30-35% est.): batteries themselves, inverters, controls
  - Operations & Maintenance (10-15% est.): facility operation, maintenance, monitoring
- This breakdown is DIFFERENT from battery CELL manufacturing focus
- Stage-5 claims SCOPE-04 and UNDR-01 use IREC data to clarify that "52.3% construction" is not applicable to cell manufacturing (only to storage system deployment)

**Why this matters:**
- Some policy discussions conflate battery CELL manufacturing workforce with battery STORAGE deployment workforce
- IREC data shows storage workforce is heavily construction (temporary jobs at deployment sites)
- Cell manufacturing workforce is mostly permanent manufacturing + engineering roles
- Helps explain why some estimates seem inflated (mixing construction jobs with manufacturing jobs)

**Cross-references:**
- Distinct from C2ES report which cites 36% construction for FULL battery supply chain
- IREC 52.3% applies specifically to storage deployment, not cell manufacturing
- Used in Stage-5 to separate scope: UNDR-01 claims construction is NOT primary lever for cell manufacturing employment

**Methodology:**
- IREC surveys multiple state workforce agencies, utility databases, company reports
- Self-reported employment by companies in clean energy sectors
- Aggregated to national totals with sector breakdowns

**Caveats:**
- "Battery storage" includes stand-alone (grid-scale) and residential (rooftop) systems
- Residential construction may inflate percentage (labor-intensive per KWh)
- Grid-scale storage construction may have different labor intensity than cell manufacturing
- Survey may over/under-count based on response rates
