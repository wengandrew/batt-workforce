## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- Voit Co. is a site-selection consulting firm specializing in industrial facility location
- 2025 analysis based on client engagements and facility selection decisions
- Key insight: power access has become THE critical factor (surpassing traditional criteria like labor, taxes)
- Highly relevant to battery manufacturing (energy-intensive, 10-20x typical industrial power demand)

**Why power access matters for batteries:**
- Battery cell manufacturing is extremely energy-intensive:
  - Heating/cooling (thermal cycling, drying ovens)
  - Precision equipment (coating, testing, robotics)
  - Required reliability (24/7 production, no power interruptions)
- Power costs are 5-15% of cell manufacturing cost (varies by region and technology)
- Power reliability is critical: blackouts halt production, incur massive losses

**Ranking vs. other site factors:**
- Traditional hierarchy (pre-2020): labor > land cost > tax incentives > power
- New hierarchy (2025): power > labor ≈ logistics > land cost > tax incentives
- Shift reflects: (1) EV/battery demand for large baseload power, (2) grid constraints in US/EU, (3) rising electricity costs post-energy crisis

**Implications for regional concentration:**
- REGION-01 claim: power access (not tax incentives) determines optimal site selection
- Suggests battery manufacturing will concentrate in:
  - Regions with hydropower (Pacific Northwest, Canada)
  - Regions with stable, low-cost baseload (nuclear, CCGT in Texas, Southeast)
  - Regions with energy-intensive industrial traditions (Ohio, Pennsylvania)
- NOT necessarily in highest-tax-incentive regions (Tennessee, Georgia)

**Policy implications:**
- NEW-08 flag: traditional economic development strategy (tax breaks, training) may be insufficient if power access is constrained
- Suggests regional winners/losers are partially determined by power infrastructure
- Implies workforce in power-constrained regions may grow slower than job announcements suggest

**Cross-references:**
- Complements Ford BlueOval (ford_authority_blueoval) which selected Tennessee partly for power access to TVA
- Relates to CSIS analysis (csis_new_phase_2026) of facility risk: power access may explain regional cancellations
- Supports market_share sensitivity: regional distribution driven more by power than by policy

**Caveats:**
- Voit commentary is based on consulting firm's client base (may be biased toward large, sophisticated clients)
- Power access is necessary but not sufficient (labor, logistics still matter)
- Regional renewable energy expansion could change the picture (solar/wind in high-incentive regions)

**Methodology limitations:**
- Voit analysis is practitioner-based, not quantitative modeling
- Does not provide detailed data on power cost/reliability by region
- No ranking of secondary factors (labor vs. logistics vs. land)
