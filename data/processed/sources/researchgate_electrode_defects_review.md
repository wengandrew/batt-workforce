## Extracted Quotes / Claims

> "A defect introduced in the first few steps gets propagated through all the steps of manufacturing through the entire line."

> "Electrode production, which is the focus of this work, accounts for approximately 39% [of cell assembly costs]... quality assurance criteria are needed... To ensure efficient production of high quality, yet affordable battery cells, while making the best use of available raw materials and processes."

> "Understanding these relationships is urgently needed for developing effective inline quality assurance to safely reject defective electrodes that affect cell lifetime or safety, or to effectively make process adjustments to reduce scrap rates."

> "Intermediate products exhibiting non-critical defects or deviations from the norm may be further processed and later commercialized as second-grade good rather than scraped. Critical defects (e.g., responsible for severe performance or safety issues) must however be rooted out as soon as possible."

## Notes

Full research review now accessible. Provides granular technical assessment of defect types, detection methods, and criticality thresholds across electrode manufacturing (mixing, coating, drying, calendering). Documents that most existing research lacks comprehensive quantitative tolerance criteria, indicating a knowledge gap that skilled quality engineers must fill through experimental and process control work. The emphasis on inline quality assurance and the distinction between critical and non-critical defects supports workforce demand for advanced quality personnel (metrology specialists, process control engineers, quality gate operators) who can make real-time accept/reject decisions at scale.
