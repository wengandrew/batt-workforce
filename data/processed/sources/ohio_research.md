That level of growth, nearly a 30% increase on our current automotive manufacturing sector workforce, will require our industrial and academic sectors to fully recalibrate our learning pathways, scale training and skilling opportunities, and increase this new workforce to power our advanced manufacturing future."

Ford's new EV manufacturing plant in Lorain County will add an additional 1,800, and SEMCORP's new separator film facility will bring another 1,200 jobs. > "Ohio is the largest automotive parts producer and have the second-largest automotive workforce in the U.S. The push to electrify transport has already attracted more than $8 billion in auto and advanced mobility related private investments across Ohio in the last year and a half."

> "Recent investments in the state, topping nearly $10 billion, signal our rise as a global hub of innovation and manufacturing."

> "Northeast Ohio is critical to Ohio's growing AAM economy. Half of Ohio's AAM job demand is attributed to Northeast Ohio, largely driven by a steady pipeline of $3.8B of EV investments. These investments -- most notably Ford's EV Assembly Plant expansion (1.8k jobs) and Ultium's new Battery Manufacturing Plant (1.1k jobs) – drive demand for engineers, equipment technicians, and credentialed operators."

## Notes

**Publisher and source authority:**
Official state strategic planning document co-published by Ohio's Governor's Office of Workforce Transformation (OWT) and Ohio Manufacturers' Association (OMA) with management consulting firm Accenture as the primary analyst. Developed through Winter 2023 stakeholder convening involving 70+ organizations spanning industry, government, community colleges, workforce development organizations, and training providers. This is a Tier 2 credibility source due to direct state government authorship and grounding in primary stakeholder engagement.

**Key battery/EV workforce findings:**

1. **25,000 new jobs projected by 2030 in Ohio** across EV manufacturing, battery development, charging infrastructure, and vehicle maintenance; representing a ~30% increase to the current automotive manufacturing workforce baseline. This is the primary quantitative anchor for the state.

2. **Regional concentration in Northeast Ohio**: Half of the statewide job demand (12.7k of 25k+ total) is concentrated in Northeast Ohio, driven by three anchor investments:
   - Honda + LG Energy Solution battery plant (Fayette County): 2,200 jobs
   - Ford EV Assembly Plant expansion: 1,800 jobs (primarily 1.8k in Northeast region)
   - Ultium Battery Manufacturing Plant: 1,100 jobs
   - SEMCORP separator film facility: 1,200 jobs
   - Other investments: $3.8B steady pipeline in Northeast alone

3. **Labor intensity observation**: Document explicitly notes that **battery manufacturing requires greater percentage of labor hours than ICE powertrains** due to the battery and unique EV-specific modules (battery management systems, on-board chargers, electric motors, DC/DC converters). Process steps and cycle times (not just automation capability) determine skill and labor hours required — directly supporting manufacturing_productivity sensitivity.

4. **Skill requirements and gap analysis**: Report identifies critical skill shortages in:
   - Electrician apprenticeships (only 156 completions in Northeast Ohio in 2022 vs. high demand)
   - Technical degree graduates
   - Engineering roles (engineers are in surplus; technicians are the bottleneck)
   - Battery-specific technical expertise and cell manufacturing operations

5. **Six-region stratification** (per JobsOhio regions):
   - Northeast Ohio (high demand, 12.7k): Ford + Ultium + existing manufacturing ecosystem
   - Central Ohio (moderate demand, 4.0k): Intel mega-site (3,000 jobs non-battery) plus EV expansion
   - Southwest Ohio (foundational, 1.7k): EV maintenance and EVSE installation focus
   - Southeast Ohio (moderate-to-low demand): Ultium Cells and Foxconn partnerships, limited existing capacity
   - Northwest Ohio (low): EV maintenance and charging infrastructure
   - Appalachian Ohio (emerging): Limited current demand but "room to grow"

6. **Training ecosystem assessment**: Report inventoried existing training programs across 4-year universities, community colleges, technical colleges, and apprenticeship providers. Found:
   - Hundreds of vacant AAM training spots annually despite high employer demand
   - Strong programs at Sinclair Community College (Tesla START Program, only Midwest provider)
   - Weak linkage between training capacity and regional employer needs
   - Need for curriculum alignment across institutions

7. **Supply-demand imbalance**: Significant supply-demand gap in skilled trades; engineering supply exceeds demand while electrician and technical trades face shortages. Report flags mismatch between education pipeline (heavy toward 4-year degrees) and actual employer needs (heavy toward 2-year technical credentials and apprenticeships).

8. **Automation vs. labor demand paradox**: Despite significant investment in automation and Industry 4.0 technologies, the report emphasizes that process optimization, quality control, predictive maintenance, and systems troubleshooting will sustain high labor demand through the ramp-up phase (2025-2032). This supports both automation and manufacturing_productivity sensitivities.

9. **Policy anchors**: 
   - Federal funding: Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) investment tax credits (45X, 48C) and clean vehicle tax credits (30D)
   - State investment: $8B+ in announced private investment (as of 2023); state workforce development through OWT and JobsOhio coordination
   - Regional approach: Multi-agency coordination model with school districts, community colleges, and industry sector partnerships (ISPs)

**Sensitivity assignments:**

1. **manufacturing_productivity** (primary): Labor intensity of battery vs. ICE manufacturing; process step analysis; skill/labor hour determination; ramp-up vs. steady-state dynamics
2. **automation** (secondary): Predictive maintenance workforce roles; quality control and process data interpretation as automation-resistant labor demand; human-machine interface upskilling
3. **policy_scenarios** (secondary): Tied to IRA incentives, state workforce coordination funding, and regional economic development policy (JobsOhio, DriveOhio)
4. **market_share** (tertiary): Regional concentration of job demand; facility-by-facility investment tracking (Honda/LG, Ford, Ultium, SEMCORP); state-level market share in North American battery manufacturing

**Limitations and caveats:**

1. Report published June 2023 (data snapshot through early 2023); some investment timelines may have shifted since publication
2. 25,000 job projection through 2030 represents a "base case" under continued federal policy support (IRA); assumes sustained incentive funding and state co-investment
3. Supply-side projections draw on existing education/training program data; do not account for rapid curriculum scaling or federal workforce development grants that may have accelerated training capacity post-publication
4. Regional demand assumes facility investments actualize on-schedule; construction delays or investment deferrals would reduce projections
