## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- Hannah Ritchie (CRU Group analyst) comparative analysis of labor intensity across regions
- Compares BYD (China's largest battery manufacturer) ~50 w/GWh to US average ~315 w/GWh
- 6x spread is the single largest "automation effect" identified in Stage-5 research
- Highlights the role of production experience, automation investment, and labor cost arbitrage

**What drives the 6x spread:**
- BYD factors: 15+ years manufacturing experience, massive scale (world's largest), lowest labor costs, state support for automation, focused on LFP (simpler than NCA/NMC)
- US factors: newer facilities (2020+), smaller individual plants, higher labor costs, ICE legacy workforce, mix of chemistries (NMC, LFP, solid-state pipeline)
- 6x is NOT just labor cost — includes process maturity, automation depth, and scrap rate differences

**Cross-references:**
- BYD ~50 w/GWh is at the lower bound of cell_labor_intensity_dataset_2026 band (steady 72-87), suggesting frontier/best-practice
- US ~315 w/GWh is FULL CHAIN (mining, refining, cell, pack, recycling) per upjohn_naatbatt_2023, not cell-only
- Ritchie's 315 likely conflates cell tier with broader supply chain; core cell manufacturing should be ~100-120 w/GWh in US
- CRU Ritchie figures useful for directional insight but require careful denominator verification

**Implications for automation sensitivity:**
- AUTO-01 claim anchors on this 50 vs 315 comparison to frame automation potential
- If US automation improves toward 150-180 w/GWh (European standard), labor intensity could drop 50-60%
- But BYD's 50 reflects both automation AND massive scale + experience; US won't match without 15+ years OR wholesale technology leapfrog

**Caveats and limitations:**
- BYD figure may reflect only cell tier, not verified for full scope
- US 315 conflates multiple value-chain segments; cell manufacturing alone is ~95-180 w/GWh
- Ritchie's analysis is published in substack (blog), not peer-reviewed
- Labor cost per hour differs 3-4x between China and US, reducing direct comparability

**Methodology uncertainty:**
- Source of BYD and US figures not fully specified in analyst commentary
- May reflect average across multiple facilities vs. specific facility data
- No mention of capacity utilization adjustments
