## Notes

Single-page summary of labor intensity across the battery manufacturing lifecycle. This is a highly condensed data sheet documenting the key finding that cell manufacturing labor intensity is dynamic: high during ramp-up (>400 workers/GWh), declining sharply at steady-state (80-143 workers/GWh depending on facility maturity and automation).

- **Data source credibility**: Compiled from publicly-available sources (company reports, press releases, news) and University of Michigan independent research project; production capacity reflects "realized or deployed capacity, not promised capacity" — conservative estimate.
- **Workforce trajectory insight**: Implies significant automation and process improvement path from ramp-up to mature production; critical for modeling View A (labor intensity curve).
- **Regional variation**: LGES Poland (81) appears to achieve lowest labor intensity; LGES US (108) and PENA US (137) are mid-range; suggests labor cost or automation differences by geography.

**Scope limitation**: Data limited to cell manufacturing; does not cover module/pack assembly, supply chain support, or non-manufacturing roles.

**Overlap with Battery Talent Census**: Census provides soft skills, training, and workforce composition insights; this document provides facility-level labor intensity quantification. Complementary data sets.

**Follow-up extraction ideas**: Would benefit from ramp-up vs. steady-state labor intensity curves with timeline annotations; comparison to historical automotive cell manufacturing ramp timelines; annotation of which facilities are largely automated vs. labor-intensive processes.
