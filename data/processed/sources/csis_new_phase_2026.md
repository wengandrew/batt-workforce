## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- CSIS Analysis of US battery manufacturing sector (2026 assessment)
- Assesses post-OBBBA policy shock (Section 45X survived, but with stricter requirements and 2030-2033 phase-out)
- Three critical findings:
  1. US is small (5%) but growing share of global capacity
  2. Risk is HIGH — many announced projects at risk of cancellation
  3. 2025 showed negative investment momentum (more cancellations than new projects)

**Market share finding:**
- US 5% of global battery capacity (2026) is lower bound estimate
- Pre-OBBBA, targets were 10-15% (US high scenario in global_jobs.json)
- 5% reflects delayed/cancelled projects from policy uncertainty

**Facility closure risk - 29-72% range:**
- Lower bound (29%): conservative scenario, only highest-risk projects cancelled
- Upper bound (72%): severe scenario, many marginal projects cancelled
- Most likely: 40-50% of announced capacity may not materialize by 2035
- Driven by: subsidy reduction (45X phaseout 2030-2033), rising costs, EV demand uncertainty

**Investment churn finding:**
- 2025 H1-H2: cancellations of ~$11B exceed new announcements of ~$8B
- Net negative $3B signals loss of confidence in US as manufacturing hub
- Contrast to 2021-2023 when IRA drove announcement boom (+$40B/yr)
- Suggests policy uncertainty (OBBBA, potential tariffs, labor cost inflation) is suppressing investment

**Implications for workforce modeling:**
- SCEN-02 claim: US capacity at risk means labor demand may be 29-72% lower than announced
- Stage-5 research uses CSIS data to reduce US market share projections from 10-15% (IRA high) to 7-10% (post-OBBBA realistic)
- NEW-04 flag: "2025 investment churn" signals emerging risk to US battery workforce buildout

**Cross-references:**
- US 5% global share aligns with market_share/regional_share.csv (US capacity post-OBBBA ~5%)
- Connects to millerchevalier_obbba_45x analysis of credit changes
- Supports conservative demand scenario (mckinsey_demand_outlook_2025 fading momentum)

**Caveats and uncertainties:**
- CSIS analysis based on public announcements (not facility-level data)
- Closure risk percentages are CSIS estimates, not hard commitments
- 2025 data is recent; subsequent policy actions (tariffs, additional tax breaks) could shift trajectory
- Chinese competition and supply chain resilience not fully modeled

**Methodology:**
- CSIS tracked announced battery projects and associated capex
- Cross-referenced with facility-level progress (construction timelines, hiring, equipment orders)
- Built risk model based on project economics (capex, subsidy dependency, EV demand)
