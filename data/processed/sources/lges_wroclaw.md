## Notes

**Scope and significance:**
- LG Energy Solution (LGES) is the world's second-largest battery manufacturer
- Wroclaw (Poland) facility is a major European cell production site
- Two-point labor intensity series covering facility lifecycle: startup (2020) to steady-state (2024)
- Critical case study for understanding ramp-up vs. steady-state labor dynamics

**Labor intensity progression:**
- 2020 (startup): 464 workers/GWh — high ramp-up phase labor intensity
- 2024 (maturity): 81 workers/GWh — stable steady-state
- Reduction factor: 464/81 ≈ 5.7x improvement over 4 years
- 81 w/GWh falls at the lower end of the 72-87 band in cell_labor_intensity_dataset_2026

**What drove the improvement:**
- Process optimization and operator skill accumulation
- Automation deployment over facility lifetime
- Increased capacity utilization (reduced scrap rates as manufacturing improves)
- Knowledge transfer from LGES global operations
- Steady increase in production volume allowing for specialization

**Cross-references:**
- Consistent with the ramp (464) vs. steady (81) band separation finding in Stage-5
- Similar pattern visible in LGES Michigan 108 w/GWh (slightly higher than Poland, suggesting US labor cost premium)
- Distinct from Weng et al. assembly-tier study which shows 10x+ labor intensity in EV assembly during ramp-up

**Implications for global workforce projection:**
- This five-year trajectory is relatively mature for European plants
- Other 2020-2023 startup facilities are still in the 125-188 ramp-up band
- As more plants mature (2026-2030), aggregate global labor intensity will fall toward 72-87 steady-state
- However, ramp-up phase plants coming online simultaneously offset some maturation gains

**Caveats:**
- Poland labor costs are lower than US but higher than China, so Wroclaw may not be globally representative
- 81 w/GWh uses official LGES employment and production figures (relatively transparent for a major OEM)
- Facility may have optimized processes specific to European cost structure, not transferable to all regions
