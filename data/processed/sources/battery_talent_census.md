## Notes

Survey-based intelligence on skilled knowledge workers (97% of respondents held a bachelor's degree or higher) in the battery industry. Key findings:

- **Skills gaps**: Manufacturing scale-up, process engineering, testing/failure analysis, quality control, and electrochemistry are top-demand areas; formal training structures underutilized.
- **Workforce composition**: Highly educated (74% with master's degrees, 39% with doctorates), STEM-dominated (90%), predominantly male (80%), international (36% of US professionals non-citizens; 60% of students).
- **Career dynamics**: High mobility (50% actively seeking roles, 2.3-year avg tenure), but on-the-job training dominates (90%) with slow onboarding (78% >3 months).
- **Training needs**: 65% of respondents cite need for better foundational knowledge; 45% cite hands-on skills gaps; formal battery-specific curricula lacking.
- **Gender equity gaps**: Women underpaid by up to 30% at entry levels; less than half of battery professionals report compensation satisfaction despite 23% wage premium over adjacent industries.

**Note on scope**: This census covers knowledge workers (engineers, scientists, business leaders) not skilled trades workers (production operators, technicians). Trades workforce gaps not captured here but equally critical.

**Overlap note**: Battery Report 2025 Talent section may contain some overlapping data but appears to be a separate summary document. This census is the most comprehensive workforce skills/training inventory available for the battery industry.

**Follow-up extraction ideas**: Detailed occupation breakdown by value-chain segment (mining vs. cell vs. pack vs. recycling) could be extracted from detailed tables; compensation/benefits data could inform manufacturing_productivity sensitivity modeling; international talent flow data (36% non-citizens) relevant to policy_scenarios.
