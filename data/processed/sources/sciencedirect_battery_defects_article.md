## Extracted Quotes / Claims

> "Currently, scrap rates in battery production are reported to range between 5% and 30% or even more, depending on the manufacturer. Especially during the ramp-up of production and introduction of innovative processes and machinery, the scrap-rate can be very high."

> "It's reported that the scrap rate should be maintained below 10% to ensure profitability in battery manufacturing plants... manufacturing scraps will still be the primary source for recycling in this decade."

> "The coating process is the main contributor to the high rejection rates in current battery cell manufacturing. Therefore, the primary focus should be on improving the coating process to reduce rejection. The implementation of data-driven methodologies could be a potential solution to improve the coating process."

> "Any few percent scrap rate would correspond to a loss of several billions of dollars, assuming the current average cell cost of 140 $/kWh."

## Notes

Full article now accessible. Provides concrete quantification of manufacturing scrap generation across ramp-up and steady-state phases, with critical focus on coating process as the dominant source of defects. Documents the economic magnitude of scrap (billions annually at scale) and identifies data-driven process improvement as a key lever for reducing workforce burden on quality/rework operations during ramp-up. The article distinguishes battery scraps from spent batteries and emphasizes that manufacturing scraps will dominate recycling volumes through 2030, implying sustained workforce demand for scrap management, recycling, and process optimization roles in gigafactories.
