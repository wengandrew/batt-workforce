# National Guideline Standards for Battery Machine Operator

## Overview

The Battery Workforce Initiative (BWI) conducted extensive analysis of battery production tasks to establish standardized skills, knowledge, and abilities requirements for two key roles in the industry.

## Battery Machine Operator Skills

The operator role requires competency across twelve key areas:

"Safety, Mixing, Electrode prep, Winding, Deep can welding, Laser welding, E-filling, Notching, Stacking, Degassing and sealing, Formation, Basic soft skills"

## Battery Machine Repair Technician Skills

This more advanced role encompasses a broader scope, including:

Safety fundamentals, industry process knowledge, blueprint interpretation, diagnostic equipment usage, machine operation across multiple production stages, preventive maintenance protocols, troubleshooting and repair for mechanical systems, welding capabilities, machining calculations, and professional interpersonal skills.

## Training & Apprenticeship Structure

The repair technician program covers "battery industry fundamentals, safety, confined space, industrial math, blueprint reading, rigging, trouble shooting and preventive maintenance, machining, welding and advanced system repair."

Organizations can structure this as a four-year, 8,000-hour time-based apprenticeship supplemented by 608 hours of related technical instruction, delivered through classroom, online, and on-site formats.
