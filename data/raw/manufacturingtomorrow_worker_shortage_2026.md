# U.S. Manufacturing Worker Shortage: Key Findings

**The Core Crisis**
A 2026 report from MIE Solutions reveals that U.S. manufacturing faces a significant labor gap. With approximately 26% of the current industrial workforce eligible for retirement, the sector anticipates between 1.5 to 2 million vacant positions by the early 2030s.

**Job Posting Intensity Metrics**
The report introduced a benchmark measuring manufacturing job postings against Bureau of Labor Statistics employment data. While the national score stands at 0.50, nearly half of U.S. states exceed this threshold, indicating substantial hiring pressure above average levels.

**Regional Disparities**
Virginia emerges as the most strained labor market, with a job posting intensity score of 2.83 per 1,000 jobs—"5.6 times the national average." This pressure stems from major facility investments and defense contracts. Other challenged states include Montana (1.14), Vermont (0.97), New Hampshire (0.94), and New Mexico (0.72).

**Employment Trends**
Factory employment has declined more than 70,000 positions since April 2025, reaching 12.69 million by December—the lowest level since March 2022. The Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index contracted for ten consecutive months, remaining below the critical 50-point threshold throughout 2025.

**Skills Gaps**
High-demand occupations include CNC machining, maintenance technicians, and process engineering roles, reflecting the industry's need for specialized expertise.
