# Georgia Worker Training and EV Battery Plant Incentives

## Worker Training Achievements

Georgia's Quick Start program is positioned as the nation's premier workforce training initiative. The program has been ranked "#1 workforce training program 13 years in a row," according to GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson. Quick Start operates by visiting company facilities internationally to understand operations firsthand, then building training stations that mirror actual workstations rather than simulating them.

**Key Quote:** "We don't just simulate what the job will be. We literally replicate it." — Rodger Brown, Quick Start Executive Director

The program has operated since 1968 and was instrumental in preparing workers for major manufacturing operations. When Kia announced its West Point facility in 2006, Quick Start deployed immediately to create training programs before the company formally broke ground.

## EV Battery Plant Incentives and Outcomes

**SK Battery America (Commerce, Georgia)**
- Announced: November 2018
- Investment: $1.67 billion
- Initial job target: 2,600 employees
- Status: Operating ahead of schedule and "almost at target" according to company leadership

**Additional EV Sector Projects:**
- Rivian: $5 billion plant near Social Circle/Madison
- Hyundai Motor Group: $5.5 billion for battery and EV plants in Savannah
- FREYR Battery: $2.57 billion battery plant in Newnan

The article emphasizes Georgia's location advantages, particularly proximity to Atlanta's workforce population base within a 40-mile radius.
