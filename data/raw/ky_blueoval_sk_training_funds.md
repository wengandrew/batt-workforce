# Kentucky Approves $15.2 Million in Workforce Training Funding

## BlueOval SK Battery Park Investment

Governor Andy Beshear announced approval of over $15.2 million in state support for workforce training initiatives across Kentucky, with a significant portion dedicated to BlueOval SK Battery Park in Glendale.

## Key Funding Details

BlueOval SK received a $10 million grant to train approximately 5,000 employees at the facility, which is scheduled to begin operations in 2025. This approval aligns with Ford and SK On's $5.8 billion investment announced in 2021, which committed to creating 5,000 full-time jobs in the state.

According to Governor Beshear: "This funding will help train workers at BlueOval SK Battery Park in Glendale and roughly 20 other operations statewide" to prepare workers for skilled, well-compensated positions.

## Training Curriculum

The training program will cover industrial maintenance, lean manufacturing, safety protocols, process improvement, systems thinking, communication skills, and battery technology understanding—critical expertise for EV battery production.

## Supporting Infrastructure

A $25 million ECTC BlueOval SK Training Center near the battery park site is under construction and scheduled for completion in 2024. This co-branded facility represents a partnership between the community college system and the manufacturing operation.

## Additional Approvals

Beyond the BlueOval SK funding, the Bluegrass State Skills Corporation approved over $1.4 million to support training for approximately 8,868 employees across 19 additional projects statewide, plus $3.8 million in Metropolitan College Tax Credits benefiting nearly 1,700 students.
