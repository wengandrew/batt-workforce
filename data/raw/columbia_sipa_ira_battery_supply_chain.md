# The IRA and the US Battery Supply Chain: One Year On

**Source**: Center on Global Energy Policy at Columbia University  
**Authors**: Ahmed Mehdi, Tom Moerenhout  
**Date**: September 20, 2023

## Key Quantitative Claims

**Investment & Capacity:**
- Over $110 billion in clean energy investments followed the IRA's passage
- Approximately $70 billion earmarked specifically for US battery supply chains
- US gigafactory capacity in pipeline increased from ~700 GWh (July 2022) to 1.2 TWh (July 2023)

**Manufacturing Costs:**
- 2022 US production costs: $135/kWh for NCM 811 cells; $125/kWh for LFP cells
- China's capex intensity: ~$60 million/GWh vs. US average of ~$90 million/GWh
- IRA tax credits reduce costs by approximately $45/kWh

**Regional Distribution:**
- Approximately 80% of clean-tech and semiconductor investments announced in Republican-leaning states
- Major concentrations in South Carolina, Georgia, Michigan, and Ohio

**Potential Fiscal Impact:**
- Production tax credit costs could reach approximately $150 billion by 2032
- Tesla potentially eligible for up to $45 billion in tax credits through 2032

**Supply Chain Reality:**
- Approximately 96% of high-purity manganese sulfate refining capacity currently located in China
