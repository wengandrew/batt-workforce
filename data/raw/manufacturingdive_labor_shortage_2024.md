# Manufacturing Labor Shortage: Key Findings

## Primary Claims

**Workforce Gap:** According to a Deloitte and Manufacturing Institute report, manufacturers could need 3.8 million new workers by 2033, with approximately 1.9 million positions potentially unfilled if current labor gaps persist.

**Current Employment:** The industry employed 13 million workers as of January 2024, with manufacturing establishments increasing by over 11% between early 2019 and mid-2023 to reach 393,000 facilities.

## Critical Skill Demands

**Technical Roles Growth:** Job posting data revealed a 75% surge in demand for simulation and related software skills, primarily for technology-enabled production positions. Industrial machinery maintenance technician positions are projected to increase 16% by 2032.

**Education Mismatch:** Certificate awards grew more than four times faster than associate degrees for manufacturing programs between 2011-2022, potentially creating gaps for advanced technical roles requiring bachelor's or associate's degrees in computer science and engineering.

## Retention Strategies

Companies prioritize:
- "Competitive benefits" as the top attraction method
- Flexible work arrangements and shift options
- Internal training and mentoring programs
- Partnerships with community organizations and educational institutions

## Workforce Expansion Recommendations

Manufacturing Institute leadership emphasized the need to "broaden the aperture" by recruiting underrepresented populations including pandemic workforce dropouts, refugees, formerly incarcerated individuals, and people with cognitive disabilities.
