# US EV Battery Belt: Key Claims

## Geographic Region

The article identifies the Battery Belt as "a geographical region that roughly runs between Michigan to Tennessee to Georgia to western New York." Gigafactories cluster in the Midwest and South, plus near Tesla facilities in California and Texas.

## Investment Scale

Since early 2021, over 15 new battery gigafactories or expansions have been announced, representing "a potential investment of at least $40 billion" according to the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas. Nearly all exceed 10 GWh capacity, with the largest exceeding 40 GWh.

## Production Growth

US battery capacity is projected to "grow more than fivefold from 2021 to 2026" and expand an additional 86% by 2031, based on Benchmark Mineral Intelligence data.

## Major Automaker Commitments

- Ford: $50 billion through 2026 for EV expansion
- GM: $35 billion through 2025, plus a $2.5 billion DOE loan with LG for three gigafactories creating 11,100 jobs

## Rationale for Clustering

Batteries are expensive to transport due to strict safety regulations, making domestic manufacturing near automakers economically sensible from a supply chain perspective.
