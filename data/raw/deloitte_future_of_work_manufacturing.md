# The Future of Work in Manufacturing

## Key Claims About the Manufacturing Workforce

**Job Creation vs. Destruction**
The article asserts that "technology is likely to create more jobs than it destroys—as it has done historically," contradicting predictions of widespread job losses from automation.

**Skills Gap Crisis**
"Deloitte and The Manufacturing Institute anticipate the shortfall in US manufacturing during the next decade to reach the highest levels ever recorded, higher than the earlier estimates of 2 million unfilled jobs."

## Emerging Skilled Roles

The report introduces ten future manufacturing personas representing 2025 job archetypes:

- Digital Twin Engineer
- Predictive Supply Network Analyst
- Robot Teaming Coordinator
- Smart Factory Manager
- Drone Data Coordinator
- Smart QA Manager

## Critical Skill Transformations

**"Soft" Skills Emphasis**
As routine tasks become automated, uniquely human competencies gain importance. The article cites research finding that "critical thinking, creativity, and people management" rank among essential capabilities for the coming decade.

**Human-Technology Integration**
Organizations are "putting humans in the loop—rethinking work architecture, retraining people, and rearranging the organization to leverage technology to transform business."

## Core Themes

The transformation emphasizes blending advanced technologies with digital literacy and interpersonal skills to maintain workforce competitiveness in Industry 4.0.

## Workforce Development Statement

"We see that workforce demand remains substantial overall, though the challenge is a shift from lower skill, repetitive, traditional production-line type roles (replaced by automation) to higher skilled roles. This is also where we see the largest gap in the workforce (skilled trade or journeyman level roles - maintenance, controls technician, manufacturing tech, production tech - increasingly more demand with a depleting workforce). State level workforce development programs in the US are starting to catch up with this need..."
