# Center for Automotive Research (CAR) — BIETNA Battery Industry & EV Technology North America Report

**Source**: Center for Automotive Research (funded by Argonne National Laboratory and Li-Bridge)  
**Report**: Battery Industry Education and Training Needs Assessment (BIETNA)  
**Accessed**: 2026-06-11

## Key Findings

The BIETNA identified critical challenges facing battery manufacturers:

- **Skills Deficiencies**: The industry faces "significant skills gaps" that threaten production capacity
- **Labor Shortages**: Workforce availability cannot currently match industry growth projections
- **Talent Retention**: Companies struggle with recruitment and keeping experienced employees
- **Training Deficits**: Educational programs lag behind employer requirements
- **Largest Shortages**: Upstream supply chain (mining, refining), followed by downstream (cell manufacturing, pack assembly)
- **Critical Skills Shortage**: Chemistry, chemical engineering, battery management systems, and graduate-level technical competencies

## Recommended Solutions

The assessment proposes several interventions:

1. Expand educational and training initiatives targeting industry-specific competencies
2. Strengthen coordination between academic institutions and employers to align curriculum with job market needs
3. Scale apprenticeship models providing practical, on-the-job training
4. Establish supportive policies promoting workforce development initiatives

## Context

Demand for lithium batteries is accelerating due to electric vehicle adoption and renewable energy storage expansion. Addressing workforce gaps is deemed essential to sustaining North America's domestic battery supply chain competitiveness.
