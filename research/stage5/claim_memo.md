# Stage 5 — Claim Memo

**Project:** Battery Workforce Whitepaper — *How Many Workers Are Needed for Battery Manufacturing?*
**Organization:** Volta Foundation · Research & Insights
**Stage:** 5 — Final Research Package
**Date:** June 12, 2026

For each major claim: recommended status (controlled vocabulary), best supporting evidence, best counterevidence, evidence classification, confidence, public-use readiness, triangulation status, review status (Stage-4 verdict), review qualification, and notes for final drafting.

**Recommended-status vocabulary:** use strongly / use with qualification / use only as hypothesis / internal framing only / background only / omit / contradicted.
**Public-use readiness:** Ready / Ready with qualification / Not yet public-ready / Internal only.
**Triangulation:** ≥2 independent credible sources (Triangulated) / Single credible source (Single-source) / Derived/inferred (Derived).

### SCOPE-01 — Workforce figures rarely state their scope boundary
- **Recommended status:** use strongly (as reframed)
- **Best supporting evidence:** Primary literature (ICCT, Upjohn, IEA) and the 12-source benchmark search jointly show boundary disclosure is inconsistent, concentrated in secondary/political use.
- **Best counterevidence:** Major primary sources DO declare scope — so the indictment must target secondary usage, not the field wholesale.
- **Evidence classification:** Public · **Confidence:** High · **Public-use readiness:** Ready · **Triangulation:** Triangulated
- **Review status:** Qualified · **Review qualification:** Narrow to secondary/popular use.
- **Notes for drafting:** This is the paper's framing hook. Credit primary sources for disclosure; locate the problem in how figures are *quoted*, not how they are *produced*.

### SCOPE-02 — FCAB segmentation canonical in US federal-policy context
- **Recommended status:** use with qualification
- **Best supporting evidence:** FCAB Blueprint is the reference taxonomy across US federal manufacturing policy.
- **Best counterevidence:** Li-Bridge, IEA, BNEF, and EU use non-matching taxonomies; no universal convergence.
- **Evidence classification:** Public · **Confidence:** High · **Public-use readiness:** Ready with qualification · **Triangulation:** Triangulated
- **Review status:** Qualified · **Review qualification:** Scope "canonical" to US federal policy.
- **Notes for drafting:** Adopt FCAB as the working spine but state explicitly it is one of several taxonomies.

### SCOPE-04 — Construction employment transient/separable (~52% of storage jobs during build)
- **Recommended status:** use with qualification
- **Best supporting evidence:** IREC (Nov 2025) — construction = 52.3% of battery-storage jobs.
- **Best counterevidence:** The prior ~36% figure was unsourced and ~1.5× too low; IREC's scope is storage installation, not cell mfg.
- **Evidence classification:** Public · **Confidence:** High · **Public-use readiness:** Ready with qualification · **Triangulation:** Single-source (IREC) for the magnitude; E2/BW corroborates direction
- **Review status:** Corrected · **Review qualification:** Retain "transient & separable"; replace 36% with ~52% (or scope it to cell-mfg facilities).
- **Notes for drafting:** Always separate construction from steady-state operating headcount; cite the corrected figure.

### QUANT-HEADLINE — Two tiers: cell+pack 95–180; full-chain-US ~310 (upper bound)

### ROLE-02 — Ramp intensity (125–188) exceeds steady-state (72–87, median 82), all nameplate
- **Recommended status:** use with qualification
- **Best supporting evidence:** 40-facility primary-source dataset showing band separation; LGES Poland 464→81.
- **Best counterevidence:** Weng et al. (Nat. Comms 2024) studies BEV *assembly*, not cells; ramp figures are nameplate-denominated and may be a utilization artifact; Northvolt is a distressed outlier.
- **Evidence classification:** Public · **Confidence:** Medium · **Public-use readiness:** Ready with qualification · **Triangulation:** Triangulated for band separation; Single-source for the >400 ramp peak
- **Review status:** Corrected · **Review qualification:** Drop Weng for cells; mark figures nameplate-based.
- **Notes for drafting:** State ramp>steady directionally on a nameplate basis; do NOT cite Weng's >10× for cell manufacturing. **Flagged: needs additional sources** (utilized-capacity, single-plant time series).

### QUANT-MATURITY-CURVE — Decay toward Western floor ~97 (nameplate); not a clean fitted law

- **Best counterevidence:** Provisional exponential fit R²<0 (nameplate-vs-utilized confound); few data points; CATL implies higher, models imply lower.
- **Evidence classification:** Public · **Confidence:** Low · **Public-use readiness:** Not yet public-ready (as a fitted equation) · **Triangulation:** Derived
- **Review status:** Qualified · **Review qualification:** Present ~97 as a Western floor estimate, not a universal minimum.
- **Notes for drafting:** Describe band separation, not a fitted curve; treat the equation as a methodological contribution awaiting utilized-capacity data. **Flagged: needs additional sources.**
